Traces of the past

This 40-photograph series was created as part of a cumulative documentary assignment. It captures abandoned shops in the town of Ćuprija, Serbia, left untouched for years after they ceased operating. Through the extended sequence of images, the series explores how the spirit of the past persists in the present, revealing traces of everyday life frozen in time. Each photograph contributes to a broader narrative, showing how these spaces silently preserve memories, habits, and the atmosphere of a bygone era.

Reflected perspective

Through this series, I explored my house and its surroundings through close-up photography. Each image captures the reflection of my home in a single raindrop left in the yard after rain. Within these tiny mirrored worlds, an intricate alternate universe emerges, offering a fresh perspective on familiar spaces and allowing viewers to experience everyday surroundings in a new way.

Passersby

Through this series, created as part of an exploration in documentary photography, I observed and captured random passersby in public parks. The project emerged from a desire to find common patterns among strangers, revealing an interesting repetition: people reading newspapers alone. Despite being in spaces shared with many others, each person inhabits their own private world, creating a subtle tension between solitude and public life. The series reflects on these quiet, isolated moments within everyday urban environments, offering a glimpse into the small, recurring rituals that define human behavior.

Fading presence

This black and white series explores memory and presence through long exposure photography. By capturing individuals who remained in one place before moving, the images create a ghostly transparency, evoking the lingering impression of a person within a space they once inhabited. The use of monochrome reflects the nature of recollection, which is fragmented, fleeting, and deeply personal. Each photograph serves as a meditation on the traces we leave behind and how spaces carry the subtle echoes of those who have passed through them.

Tonal childhood

This series consists of two diptychs that combine photography and hand-applied color. Four old photographs from my grandmother’s black-and-white family album, featuring her and her half-brother, were printed and reinterpreted with warm and cool tones. The warm-toned diptych represents my grandmother, whose character was affectionate and sought closeness, while the cool-toned diptych depicts her half-brother, often distant and reserved. Together, the diptychs explore the emotional contours of a shared childhood, reflecting the contrasting personalities of two people whose lives were intertwined during those formative years. Through color and photographic reinterpretation, the work meditates on memory, intimacy, and the subtle dynamics that shape familial relationships.
