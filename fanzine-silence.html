fanzine - silence
portfolio
This A6 fanzine visually interprets the experience of silence. Through illustrations of a small character, it shows how silence is gradually built as a personal space where thoughts can move freely and one feels comfortable. The front pages depict the character trapped in noise, represented by gray textures, appearing tense and static. As the fanzine progresses, silence—represented by white—slowly takes over, culminating on the back cover with the character joyfully immersed in a fully silent environment. The work captures the contrast between noise and calm, and the journey toward inner peace.
